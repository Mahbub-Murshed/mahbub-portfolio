A Mystical Journey Through Meghalaya: 5 Days of Adventure and Serenity

Home My Blog Meghalaya

In August 2022, I embarked on a 5-day adventure to Meghalaya, India, a journey that took me through the captivating landscapes of Shillong and Cherrapunji. Starting from the Tamabil border, adjacent to Bangladesh, this trip was filled with stunning natural wonders, serene valleys, and vibrant city life. The weather was perfect—cool and misty—adding a magical touch to the entire experience.

Day 1: From Tamabil Border to Cherrapunji – Into the Misty Valleys

My journey began at the Tamabil border, where I crossed into India and made my way to Dawki. The drive to Dawki was breathtaking, with the winding roads offering glimpses of the crystal-clear Dawki River. The river’s pristine waters were mesmerizing, and I couldn’t resist taking a boat ride to fully appreciate its beauty.
From Dawki, I continued to Cherrapunji, where I would spend the next two nights. Cherrapunji, known for being one of the wettest places on Earth, greeted me with a valley shrouded in clouds. It was as if a group of clouds had decided to accompany me on this journey, creating a mystical atmosphere that made everything feel surreal.

Day 2: Exploring Cherrapunji – Waterfalls and Living Root Bridges

The second day was dedicated to exploring the natural wonders of Cherrapunji. My first stop was the iconic Nohkalikai Falls, which plunged dramatically into the depths below. The view was partially obscured by clouds, adding a sense of mystery and awe to the experience.
Next, I embarked on a trek to the Double Decker Living Root Bridge, an extraordinary feat of natural architecture. The trek was challenging but rewarding, as the bridge, formed by the interweaving roots of ancient trees, stood as a testament to the ingenuity and harmony with nature of the Khasi people.
I also visited Wei Sawdong, a lesser-known but equally stunning waterfall. Its three-tiered cascade was a sight to behold, and I spent some time simply soaking in the beauty of the surroundings.

Day 3: From Cherrapunji to Shillong – The Scotland of the East

On the third day, I left Cherrapunji and headed towards Shillong, the capital city of Meghalaya. The drive itself was an adventure, with the road cutting through mist-covered hills and offering panoramic views of the lush green valleys.
Upon reaching Shillong, I was immediately struck by its blend of modernity and tradition. My first stop was Umiam Lake, a vast reservoir surrounded by rolling hills. The serene waters and cool breeze made it the perfect spot to unwind after a day of travel.
In the evening, I explored Police Bazar, Shillong’s bustling commercial hub. The vibrant atmosphere, with its mix of local shops, cafes, and street vendors, was a delightful contrast to the tranquil landscapes I had experienced earlier. Shillong’s nightlife was also a pleasant surprise, with lively pubs and live music adding to the city’s charm.

Day 4: Mawlynnong and Beyond – Asia’s Cleanest Village and More

The fourth day took me to Mawlynnong, a village renowned as the cleanest in Asia. The village’s immaculate cleanliness and eco-friendly practices were truly inspiring. Walking through Mawlynnong, I felt a deep sense of respect for the community’s dedication to preserving their environment.
From Mawlynnong, I visited several other nearby attractions, including the Nohsngithiang (Seven Sisters) Falls. This majestic waterfall, named for its seven distinct streams, was a highlight of the day. I also made a stop at Elephant Falls, another popular spot near Shillong, where the water cascaded down in three stages, surrounded by lush greenery.

Day 5: Wrapping Up in Shillong – More Waterfalls and Scenic Beauty

My final day in Meghalaya was spent visiting more of the region’s stunning waterfalls. Krang Suri Falls, with its turquoise waters, was particularly captivating. The falls were nestled in a serene forested area, making it a peaceful and picturesque spot to conclude my trip.
Before heading back to Bangladesh, I took one last drive through the scenic landscapes, retracing my route from Shillong to Dawki and then back to the Tamabil border. The journey back was filled with reflections on the incredible beauty I had witnessed and the rich cultural experiences I had enjoyed.

Reflections on Meghalaya

Meghalaya’s mystical landscapes, from the misty valleys of Cherrapunji to the vibrant streets of Shillong, left an indelible mark on me. The region’s waterfalls, living root bridges, and crystal-clear rivers were nothing short of magical. The weather in August was ideal, with the cool, misty air adding to the allure of the place.
If you’re seeking a travel destination that offers both adventure and tranquility, Meghalaya is a hidden paradise waiting to be explored. The journey from Tamabil to Shillong and Cherrapunji was filled with unforgettable moments, and I left with memories that will last a lifetime.
