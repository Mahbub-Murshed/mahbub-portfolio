Embracing the Coastal Charm of Cox's Bazar: A 6-Day Beach Retreat

Home My Blog Cox's Bazar

Cox's Bazar has always been a place close to my heart. As a lover of the beach, there's no better place in Bangladesh to escape to, and in May 2023, I found myself once again drawn to its golden sands and calming waves. Though I’ve visited countless times before, each trip brings new experiences, and this one was no exception.Known for being the world’s longest natural sea beach, Cox's Bazar holds a special place in my heart. As a beach lover, the vibrant yet calming vibe of the shoreline always draws me back, and this trip was no exception. With my wife by my side, we indulged in the sun, sand, and sea, exploring both familiar and new spots along the coast.

Day 1: Arrival at Inani and Settling into Serenity

Our journey began with a drive along the scenic Marine Drive, a stretch of road that runs parallel to the coastline, offering stunning views of the ocean. Riding bikes along this route is one of my favorite activities; the wind in my hair and the endless ocean beside me create an unparalleled sense of freedom. We headed straight to Inani Beach, where we checked into the luxurious Sea Pearl Beach Resort & Spa. Nestled right beside the beach, this resort is a haven of tranquility. The sound of the waves and the sight of the endless horizon from our room was the perfect start to our getaway.
Inani Beach, with its serene environment and less crowded shores, is a place where time seems to slow down. We spent the day walking along the beach, feeling the soft sand under our feet, and later sat by the shore at night, listening to the waves crashing gently against the shore—a simple pleasure that never fails to rejuvenate my spirit.

Day 2: Beach Hopping and Culinary Delights in Inani

The next day was dedicated to exploring the beaches around Inani. We visited several beaches, each with its unique charm—Kolatoli, Shugandha, Laboni, and Kakra among them. However, Patuartek Beach stood out for its quietness. Far from the bustling crowds, Patuartek offers a peaceful retreat where one can truly connect with nature. The silence of this beach, broken only by the occasional call of seabirds, was incredibly soothing.
When it came to food, Inani did not disappoint. We enjoyed meals at some of my favorite spots—Palongki Restaurant, Sea Mount Beach Café, and Coral Station. These places not only serve delicious food but also offer stunning views of the beach, making each meal a memorable experience. The fresh seafood, cooked with local spices, was a particular highlight, offering flavors that were both familiar and comforting.

Day 3: Transition to Cox's Bazar and Luxurious Stay at Sayeman Beach Resort

After two blissful days in Inani, we moved back to the heart of Cox's Bazar and checked into Sayeman Beach Resort at Kolatoli Beach. This resort, with its elegant design, luxurious amenities, and breathtaking views of the beach, took our experience to another level. The infinity swimming pool was a favorite spot for both of us, offering a perfect blend of relaxation and stunning ocean views. The buffet at Sayeman was a culinary delight, with a wide variety of dishes that catered to every palate.
My wife was particularly impressed with the service at Sayeman Beach Resort, where the staff went out of their way to ensure our stay was comfortable. The resort's attention to detail and their warm hospitality made our stay even more enjoyable.

Days 4-5: Exploring Cox’s Bazar and Indulging in Culinary Adventures

The remaining days in Cox's Bazar were spent revisiting some of my favorite beaches—Kolatoli, Shugandha, Laboni, and more. Each beach, though familiar, always has something new to offer. Whether it's the changing tides, the different shades of the sunset, or the diverse crowd, there’s always something that keeps me coming back.
Food is an integral part of my travel experience, and Cox's Bazar is a culinary paradise. We dined at several of my favorite restaurants—Al Goni, Poushee, Shalik, Ruposhi Bangla, and Handi. Each meal was a delightful experience, filled with the rich flavors of Bengali cuisine. However, the highlight of our culinary journey was the dessert shop 'Sugar Boulangerie & Patisserie.' Their pastries, mushroom pie, and coffee are simply the best, and no trip to Cox's Bazar is complete without a visit there. It’s a place where I can indulge my sweet tooth and unwind with a perfect cup of coffee.

Day 6: Farewell to the Beach

As our trip came to an end, I found myself once again captivated by the charm of Cox's Bazar. There’s something about this place—the endless beaches, the sound of the waves, the vibrant local culture, and the delicious food—that always makes it feel like a home away from home. Whether I’m walking along the shore at night, riding a bike on Marine Drive, or savoring a meal at one of my favorite restaurants, Cox's Bazar never fails to bring me joy.

Final Thoughts

Cox's Bazar is more than just a travel destination for me—it’s a place where I can reconnect with nature, indulge in my love for the beach, and enjoy some of the best food Bangladesh has to offer. This trip, with its mix of luxury and simplicity, adventure and relaxation, was a perfect escape. Being able to share it with my wife made it even more special. As a Bangladeshi, I take immense pride in Cox's Bazar, and I know that I will continue to return to this beautiful coastal town for years to come.
