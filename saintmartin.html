A Tranquil Escape to Saint Martin's Island, Bangladesh

Home My Blog SaintMartin

In January 2021, I embarked on a much-anticipated journey to Saint Martin's Island, a destination that holds a special place in my heart. This was my second visit to the island, and this time, I was accompanied by my childhood friends. We spent four unforgettable days exploring every corner of this beautiful island, relishing in its natural beauty and tranquil atmosphere.

The Journey Begins: From Cox's Bazar to Teknaf

Our adventure started in Cox's Bazar, from where we headed to Teknaf. We arrived at Teknaf around 7:30 a.m., just in time to catch our ship, the **Keari Sinbad**. The ship set sail at 8 a.m., and we were all filled with excitement as we embarked on the 3-hour journey to Saint Martin's Island. The sea was calm, and the morning sun bathed the ocean in a warm glow, setting the perfect mood for the trip ahead.

Arrival and Accommodation: Blue Marine Resort

We reached the island at 11 a.m. and made our way to **Blue Marine Resort**, our home for the next four days. Located close to the jetty and the bustling market, the resort was the ideal base for our island adventures. The staff were welcoming, and the rooms were comfortable, providing a perfect blend of relaxation and convenience.

Exploring the Island: Beaches, Seafood, and Adventure

One of the best things about Saint Martin's Island is its compact size, which makes it easy to explore on foot. We spent our days wandering the island, often retracing our steps just to soak in the views again. The water surrounding the island was incredibly clean and blue, and the weather was perfect for outdoor activities.
We were drawn to the **west beach**, where the scenery was particularly breathtaking. The gentle waves lapping against the shore, the golden sands, and the distant horizon made it a perfect spot for long walks and quiet contemplation. We played beach football, cycled along the sandy paths, and simply enjoyed the peaceful surroundings.
Our evenings were filled with the tantalizing aroma of BBQ. For two nights, we indulged in freshly grilled seafood, including salmon, tuna, koral, lobster, flying fish, crabs, squid, and shurma fish. The flavors were exceptional, with the BBQ salmon and koral standing out as my personal favorites. The local delicacies were a highlight of the trip, adding a delicious dimension to our island experience.

Chewra Dwip and the Last Border

A trip to Saint Martin's wouldn't be complete without visiting **Chewra Dwip**, a smaller island nearby that is often described as one of the finest in Bangladesh. We took a boat to the island and were immediately captivated by its pristine beauty. The untouched beaches, crystal-clear waters, and the sense of solitude made it a perfect escape from the world.
We also made our way to the **last border** of the island, a spot that offers a unique perspective on the surrounding seascape. Standing there, with the vast expanse of the ocean before us, we felt a deep connection to the natural world. It was a moment of reflection, a reminder of the simple joys of life.

Reflections on Saint Martin's Island

Saint Martin's Island is a paradise for those who love the sea, the sun, and the sand. Its unspoiled beauty, combined with the warmth of its people and the freshness of its seafood, makes it a must-visit destination in Bangladesh. Our four days on the island were filled with laughter, adventure, and relaxation, creating memories that will last a lifetime.
Whether you're a seasoned traveler or someone looking for a peaceful retreat, Saint Martin's Island has something to offer. For me, it's a place where time slows down, where the worries of the world fade away, and where you can truly connect with nature and those around you. I look forward to returning again someday, to once more walk its beaches, savor its flavors, and bask in its serene beauty.
