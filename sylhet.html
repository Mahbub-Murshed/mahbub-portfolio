Discovering Sylhet: A 5-Day Adventure in the Heart of Bangladesh

Home My Blog Sylhet

In November 2023, I embarked on a memorable 5-day journey to Sylhet, Bangladesh—a place I've visited several times before, but this trip was truly special. Known for its breathtaking landscapes, serene rivers, and rich cultural heritage, Sylhet never fails to captivate. This time, I made it a point to explore almost every corner of this beautiful region, from historical sites to natural wonders.

Day 1: Arrival and Spiritual Start at Dargah Gate

My journey began at the iconic Dargah Gate, a significant cultural and spiritual site in Sylhet. Visiting the Shahjalal Mazar and Shahporan Mazar was a humbling experience, as these are the resting places of two of the most revered saints in Bangladesh. The spiritual aura of these places set a peaceful tone for the rest of my trip.
After the visit, I explored the bustling local markets around Dargah Gate, soaking in the vibrant atmosphere before heading to dinner at the renowned Pach Bhai Restaurant. The flavors of traditional Sylheti cuisine, with its rich spices and unique textures, were the perfect way to end the first day.

Day 2: The Beauty of Jaflong and Bichanakandi

The second day was dedicated to exploring some of Sylhet’s most picturesque landscapes. I started with a visit to Jaflong, a stunning area where the Khasi Hills meet the plains. The sight of the crystal-clear river flowing beside the majestic hills was mesmerizing. The tranquility of Jaflong, combined with the colorful stones in the riverbed, made it a photographer’s dream.
From Jaflong, I traveled to Bichanakandi, a place where rivers from India converge, creating a serene natural pool surrounded by lush green hills. I took an engine boat to fully appreciate the beauty of Bichanakandi, with its emerald waters reflecting the surrounding greenery. Lunch at Panshi Restaurant afterward was a delicious affair, with local delicacies like bhorta and fish curry leaving a lasting impression.

Day 3: Exploring Ratargul, Lalakhal, and Keane Bridge

Day three was all about exploring Sylhet’s unique water landscapes. I started with a visit to Ratargul Swamp Forest, the only swamp forest in Bangladesh. Gliding through the calm waters on a boat, surrounded by dense greenery, was a surreal experience. The forest, partially submerged, offered a glimpse into a different world, far removed from the hustle and bustle of city life.
Next, I headed to Lalakhal, a river famous for its striking blue-green waters. The boat ride through Lalakhal was nothing short of magical, with the vibrant colors of the water contrasting beautifully with the surrounding hills. The day ended with a visit to the historic Keane Bridge, a symbol of Sylhet’s colonial past, before enjoying a sumptuous dinner at Woondal King Kabab.

Day 4: Waterfalls and Wilderness – Shongrampunji, Mayanboti, and Panthumai

The fourth day was dedicated to exploring some of Sylhet’s most beautiful waterfalls. Shongrampunji and Mayanboti were first on the list, both offering stunning views and a refreshing escape into nature. The sound of cascading water amidst the lush green surroundings was incredibly soothing, providing a perfect backdrop for relaxation and reflection.
Panthumai, another hidden gem, was next. The waterfall here was particularly breathtaking, with water flowing over smooth rocks into a clear pool below. The serenity of these places was a stark contrast to the energetic city, offering a perfect retreat into nature.

Bholaganj Shadapathor and Culinary Delights

On the final day, I visited Bholaganj Shadapathor, one of Sylhet’s hidden treasures. The white stones of Shadapathor, set against the backdrop of green hills and clear blue skies, created a surreal landscape. The boat ride through this area was peaceful and allowed me to fully take in the natural beauty of Sylhet.
Before heading back, I made sure to indulge in one last round of Sylheti cuisine, revisiting some of the best restaurants I’d enjoyed earlier. The flavors of Pach Bhai, Panshi, and Woondal King Kabab were the perfect way to wrap up a fantastic trip.

Reflections on Sylhet

Sylhet, with its rich blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage, never ceases to amaze. This 5-day journey allowed me to experience the best of what the region has to offer, from the spiritual calm of its mazars to the breathtaking landscapes of its rivers and waterfalls. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or a taste of authentic Sylheti cuisine, Sylhet is a destination that truly has it all.
